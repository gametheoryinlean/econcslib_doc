    source
    theorem SocialChoice.FairDivision.Divisible.ef_exists_two_agents (μ : Fin 2 → MeasureTheory.Measure ↑unitInterval) [MeasureTheory.IsFiniteMeasure (μ 0)] [MeasureTheory.IsFiniteMeasure (μ 1)] [MeasureTheory.NoAtoms (μ 0)] :
    ∃ (A : Allocation (Fin 2) ↑unitInterval), IsAllocation A ∧ IsEnvyFree (MeasureValuation μ) A

    EF always exists for 2 agents on the unit interval [0,1].

    This is exactly the cut-and-choose theorem specialized to an existential statement: agent 0 cuts at a fair point, agent 1 chooses their preferred side, and the resulting allocation is envy-free.
